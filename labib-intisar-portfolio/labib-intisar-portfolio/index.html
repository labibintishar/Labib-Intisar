01Profile

A planner for the climate era.

I am an Urban & Regional Planning graduate of Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET), drawn to the questions where the built environment meets environmental risk — how cities can grow without deepening their exposure to climate change, hazards, and resource scarcity.

My work moves between field study and geospatial modeling: socio-economic surveys of urban communities, satisfaction and supply-chain analyses, neighbourhood design, and geostatistical landslide-risk assessment. I care most about planning that is sustainable in the long term and equitable for the people who live with its outcomes.

As I continue toward a career in planning, my aim is to sharpen these tools further and contribute to environment-focused projects that put long-term resilience and community well-being first.

◇Geospatial Method

Reading the landscape in layers.

Every analysis I run — from rainwater-harvesting suitability in Koyra to landslide risk in the hills — overlays the same kind of spatial layers, turning raw terrain into a decision.

Animated GIS overlay · elevation → suitability
  1. 01Elevation (DEM)Surface terrain & slope from ASTER / SRTM models.
  2. 02Hydrology & DrainageFlow paths, depressions & runoff via the SCS-CN method.
  3. 03Land Use / Land CoverClassified surface and settlement patterns.
  4. 04Soil & EnvironmentSoil type, salinity and climate exposure.
  5. 05Suitability ResultWeighted overlay highlighting the optimal sites.
